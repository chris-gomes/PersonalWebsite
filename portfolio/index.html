DATA SHARING RESEARCH

As a data scientist/engineer, I know that it's important to make machine learning products the right way. With recommendation systems and self-operating devices becoming the norm, any mistakes could lead to harm to the user and others involved. One of the major questions that still has to be answered is how can these products be created in a way that does not invade or harm a user's privacy. It is a difficult problem, but, after doing the research I have done, I am confident that we can find a solution. Included below is a report of research I have done into current publications about this issue, research I have done to suggest better ways for Google to improve its thrid-party sharing policies, and a public article to show young teens how to increase the data privacy of their Google accounts.

The goal of this project was to determine if the performance of the stock market can be predicted from the public's response to events in the world.
